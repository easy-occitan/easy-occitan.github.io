III. The Languedocian Verb

The main conjugations of the Langadòc's Occitan will be given hereafter.

First and foremost we could make a comparison between some verbs endings from different Indo-European languages so as to see that knowing one language can help us to know the others.

Occitan: i/as/a/am/atz/an Portuguese: o/as/a/amos/(ais)/am
French: e/es/e/ons/ez/ent Greek: o/eis/ei/oume/ete/oun
Catalan: o/es/a/em/eu/en Russian: u/esh/et/em/ete/ut
Italian: o/i/a/amo/ate/ano Czech: u/esh/e/eme/ete/ou
Spanish: o/as/a/amos/áis/an

(Finnish (not an Indo-European language!): n/t/a/mme/tte/vat)

Hey! Easy to find similarities, isn't it? But back to our wonderful Occitan…

E. Negotiation

Basic negation is "pas", which follows the verb. Its translation is "not" ("yes" is said "òc" and "no" is said "non").

  • Ex.1: parli pas (I speak-not=I don't speak)

  • Ex.2: An pas parlat (have-not-spoken=they haven't spoken)

Pas can be combined with other negative words…

  • Ex.3: I vesi pas res (I-see-not-nothing=I don't see anything)

  • Ex.4: Lo tròbi pas enluòc (him-find-not-nowhere=I don't find him anywhere)

  • Ex.5: Sortís pas jamai (goes out-not-never=he never goes out)

  • Ex.6: Degun es pas vengut (nobody-is-not-come=nobody came)
